Vent Insulator

Coming Soon

This laser cut was made by our own design team. The design was built in fusion using parameters that were taken from Avi's own vent dimensionsIts geometrical design is engineered to reduce sound while also continuing to let air through. By adding sound proofing foam onto the walls the device further decreases noise with its smart mesh technology.

Installation Guide:
1. To make your own ventsulator with your own vent's dimensions, download the fusion file here
2. Put in your own dimensions for in the parameters tab. NB: The picture above uses the standard vent in ziv should be 39 by 9.2 cm. It is recommended to make the outside of the vent slightly bigger so it can fit around the outside
3. Download and laser print two of each of the dxf files for each sketch
4. Buy sound proofing foam
5. Glue the soundproofing foam onto all sides of the plates
6. Assemble and glue the ventsulator

*The foam should be added to the panels in the middle like so, the transparent sides are for demonstration purposes. This is compatible with Brandeis dorm rules which allow you to hold up objects with command strips or tape see the dorm rules here

Overview

Despite our idea being initially designed for Avi's vent, we realized it was much more than this. Through interviews and research, we found that many students struggle with sensory issues in their dorm but have no solution to these problems. After realizing our solution could be applied not just to Avi, we pivoted the idea so that it could become an execrable solution for any student who was struggling with sensory issues. This idea of design to improve the lives of students with disabilities is tied back to a reading by Sarah Hendrens that we did earlier in the semester. In her (2021) book, "What Can a Body Do?" Sarah Hendrens examines how technology and architecture can empower or constrain people with disabilities. She highlights how assistive devices, adaptive technologies, and inclusive design practices have improved lives and how they can help break down barriers to accessibility.
